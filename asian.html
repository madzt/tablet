Asian Recipes


Asian cuisine is influenced by major areas in Asia and is characterized by various styles and traditions. Rice is the most popular ingredient in Asian cuisine and Chinese food is the most influential dominion on United States cuisine.

Panang Curry

Do not shake coconut milk before using so that a layer of coconut cream forms at the top of the can. Heat the cream in the top of the can over high heat for about 1 minute. When the cream starts bubbling, add the curry paste and stir them to mix. Cook paste for 1-2 minutes. Next, add onion and bay leaves and stir until coated with the paste. Cook for 3-4 minutes until the onions have softened. Then, stir in the rest of the coconut milk and heat until it boils. Boil the milk until it coats the back of a spoon, 15-20 minutes. Reduce the heat to a simmer and add the chicken, lime juice, fish sauce, and sugar. Cook for 5 minutes, then add the bell pepper and zucchini. Cook for about 5 more minutes until the chicken is cooked through. Add the butter, basil, and bamboo shoots. Stir to combine everything and cook for 1 more minute. Remove the bay leaves if you can and serve over rice.

Ingredients


  • 1 can Panang curry paste (4 oz)
  • 1 can coconut milk (13.5 oz)
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1/2 cup sliced onion
  • 1 lb chicken breast, sliced into bite-sized tiles
  • 2 tsp fish sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 bell pepper sliced
  • 1 zucchini sliced
  • 1 can bamboo shoots
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1/2 chopped basil
  • 2 Bay leaves

Fried Rice

Heat vegetable oil and 1 tbsp soy sauce in a large pan over medium heat. Add chicken and cook for about 4 minutes, until lightly browned and cooked almost through. Remove chicken from pan and set aside. Heat sesame oil in the pan over medium heat and add onions. Cook onions for 3 minutes then add peas and carrots and cook for 2 more minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Next, add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Move the vegetables to one side of the pan and melt butter in the other side. Lightly beat the eggs in a bowl, then add to the buttered side of the pan. Season eggs with salt and pepper and then scramble the eggs. Once the eggs are cooked, mix with the vegetables. Add the rest of the seasonings and cook for about 1 minute. Then, add the cooked rice and chicken to the pan along with the rest of the soy sauce and the fish or oyster sauce. Stir and fry the rice and chicken for 5 minutes, until it is fully combined and the chicken is cooked through.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked rice, left in fridge overnight
  • 1/2 lb chicken breast or tenderloins, sliced into bite-sized pieces
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp fish or oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 small white or sweet onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes

Baked Lobster Roll

Preheat oven to 450 degrees Fahrenheit. Wash rice in strainer until the water runs clear. Add the rice and 2 cups water in a small pot and heat until the water boils. Once the water is boiling, turn down to a simmer and cover the rice for 15-20 minutes. In a bowl, mix the vinegar, sugar, and salt, and stir until the sugar and salt have dissolved. After the rice has cooled for 10 minutes, pour the rice into the bowl and mix it with the vinegar. In a small bowl, melt the butter and stir in salt, pepper, and garlic powder for the lobster. Brush the butter over the lobster, then bake for 12-15 minutes, until the lobster is fully cooked and no longer rubbery. Roughly chop the imitation crabmeat, and mix it with the mayo in a small bowl. Cover a sushi rolling mat with plastic wrap, then place a sheet of nori on the plastic wrap. Cover the nori with rice, wetting your hands with water before adding the rice to prevent it from sticking. Next, add 1/3 of the crab mixture in a thin line about one inch from the end of the nori sheet. Then, add 1/3 of the cucumber in a thin line next to the crabmeat and add 1/3 of the avocado in a thin line next to the cucumber. Using the rolling mat and plastic wrap, roll the nori around the filling and press to seal. Continue until you have formed a sushi roll. Cut the roll into 6 or 8 bite-sized pieces. Repeat with the other 2 nori sheets and and the rest of the filling ingredients. Lay the sushi pieces close together on a plate. Chop the baked lobster into small pieces and layer it on top of the sushi. Mix the extra mayo and sriracha in a bowl to make spicy mayo. Top sushi with eel sauce and spicy mayo.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup short grain white rice
  • 2 tbsp sushi vinegar or rice vinegar
  • 1 1/2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp salt (for rice)
  • 3 sheets nori (seaweed)
  • 1/4 lb imitation crabmeat
  • 2 tbsp mayo
  • 1/2 cucumber, peeled, seeded, julienned
  • 1/2 avocado, peeled, cut into thin strips
  • 6 oz lobster tail, cut and prepared
  • 2 tbsp melted butter
  • 1/2 tsp salt (for lobster)
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • Eel sauce for topping
  • 2 tbsp mayo (for topping)
  • 2 tsp sriracha (for topping)

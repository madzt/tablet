Italian Recipes


Italian cuisine is generally characterized by its simplicity, with many dishes having only two to four main ingredients. Its history dates back to the 4th century BC where each Roman citystate had its own approach to bread and pasta making. Italian cuisine offers an abundance of taste, and is one of the most influential cuisines on United States culinary.

Bow Tie Pasta

Bring 2 cups of water to a boil in a saucepan. Then, cook bow-tie pasta in the water for 10 minutes. Warm butter and cream in the saucepan over low heat and let it simmer for 2 minutes. Next, whisk in garlic and seasonings for 1 minute. Lastly, add parmesean cheese and stir until cheese has melted and the sauce is smooth.

Ingredients


  • 8 oz bow-tie pasta
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1 1/2 cups heavy whipping cream
  • 2 teaspoons garlic minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • 2 cups freshly grated parmesean

Penne alla vodka

Boil water in a pot. Heat olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat, then add chopped shallots and season with salt and pepper. Saute shallots for 3 minutes or until translucent. Then, melt the butter in the pan and add minced garlic. Saute garlic for 1 minute. Next, add tomato paste, red pepper flakes, and italian seasoning. Stir in the tomato paste until it coats the shallots and garlic, and cook for about 5 minutes. Then, add vodka and mix with tomato paste for 1 minute. At this point, salt your pasta water and begin cooking your pasta for 10 minutes. Turn the heat on the pan down to low and add heavy cream. Slowly bring the cream to a boil and then lower to a simmer. Allow cream to reduce for about 10 minutes, then add parmesan cheese and stir until it is completely combined with the sauce. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining pasta, and add 1/4 cup of pasta water to the sauce. If the sauce is too thin, continue to simmer until it is your desired thickness. If sauce is too thick, add more water to thin it out. Finally, stir pasta into sauce and serve with extra parmesan cheese and black pepper on top.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz penne pasta
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 1 medium shallot, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup tomato paste
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • tsp italian seasoning
  • 3 tbsp vodka
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup parmesan cheese, shredded

Bolognese

Heat oil in large saucepan or pot over medium heat. Add onion and saute for 3 minutes. Then, add carrot and celery and cook for 5 more minutes. Add garlic and cook for 1 more minute. Next, add beef and sausage and cook until brown. Add wine and milk and simmer for 10 minutes or until the liquid has mostly evaporated. Stir in the tomato paste and seasonings until the paste coats the rest of the ingredients. Then, add the tomatoes and stir. Season with salt and pepper, then cover and simmer for about 30 minutes. Boil water in a pot, then add salt and pasta and cook until pasta is done. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining and add 1/2 cup of pasta water to the sauce. Once the sauce has thickened to your desired thickness, stir in the pasta. If the sauce is too thick, add more pasta water. Serve topped with parmesan cheese and black pepper.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large white onion, finely chopped
  • 1 large carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 stalk celery, finely chopped
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1/2 lb ground italian sausage
  • 1 1/4 cups red wine
  • 1 cup milk
  • 28 oz can crushed tomatoes with juice
  • 4 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 lb pasta (tagliatelle or pappardelle)
  • 2 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 2 tsp italian seasoning
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground nutmeg
  • Parmesan cheese for topping

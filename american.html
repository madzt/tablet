American Recipes


American cuisine is a melting pot of culinary cultures from Native Americans, African Americans, Asians, Pacific Islanders, and more. It is ever expanding with the influx of immigrants from many different nations. Fun fact: French fries are the most popular American dish.

Fried Boneless Chicken Wings

In a deep pan, heat 2 inches of oil to 375 degrees Fahrenheit. In a bowl, mix flour, salt, black pepper, cayenne pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, and paprika. In another bowl, mix egg and milk. Dip each piece of chicken in the flour mixture and roll to coat, then dip in the egg mixture, and then coat again in the flour mixture. Place the chicken pieces on a baking sheet or plate and refrigerate for 20 minutes. In batches so as not to overcrowd the pan, fry chicken pieces until the outside is golden brown, 5-6 minutes. In a large pan, combine butter and hot sauce over medium heat. Add in the garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper, paprika, Worcestershire sauce, salt and pepper. Stir until the sauce is bubbling. For hotter buffalo sauce, increase the amount of hot sauce and decrease the amount of butter. Serve with blue cheese or ranch. *For milder buffalo sauce, decrease the amount of hot sauce and increase the amount of butter. Add the boneless wings to the pan and toss to coat in the sauce.

Ingredients

Buffalo Wings

  • 1 lb chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup flour
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup milk
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Buffalo Sauce

  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 2/3 cup hot sauce (Frank’s Red Hot)
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 tsp Worcestershire sauce

Spinach and Artichoke Dip

Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit. Add cream cheese, sour cream, mayo, garlic, parmesan cheese, mozzarella cheese, red pepper flakes, salt and pepper to a mixing bowl and mix until completely combined. Then stir in the artichoke hearts and spinach. Spray a small baking dish with non-stick cooking spray, then add the mixture to the dish evenly. Bake for 10 minutes, then top with extra mozzarella and parmesan cheese, and bake until cheese is bubbling and brown, 10-15 more minutes. Serve with tortilla chips.

Ingredients

  • 1 8 oz block of cream cheese
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup mayo
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2/3 cup of parmesan cheese, shredded (plus more for topping)
  • 1/2 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded (plus more for topping)
  • 14 oz artichoke hearts, chopped and squeezed to drain
  • 6 oz frozen spinach, squeezed to drain
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes

Cheese Quesadilla

Place tortilla in an oiled frying pan and set the heat to medium. Sprinkle shredded cheese evenly over the tortilla, leaving half an inch around the perimeter. Sprinkle in Green Chili Pepper, onions, and seasoning. Add hot sauce onto the cheese. Fold over tortilla and cook each side for 3 minutes. Optionally: cut into slices and serve with sour cream.

Ingredients

  • 1 large flour tortilla
  • 1 cup of shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1 tbsp chopped onions
  • 1 finely chopped Green Chili Pepper
  • 1 tsp red pepper flakes
  • hot sauce (Frank’s Red Hot)
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • (Optional) Sour Cream
